<section class="home-section home-bio">
  <h2>About Me</h2>
  <p>I'm a Research Assistant Professor at Peking University. My work focuses on AI and machine learning for biomedical data, especially neuroimaging, Alzheimer's disease diagnosis, stroke recovery prediction, and brain-computer-interface supported rehabilitation. Previously, I was a Postdoctoral Scholar in the <a href="https://loni.usc.edu/">Laboratory of NeuroImaging</a> of <a href="https://keck.usc.edu/faculty-search/arthur-w-toga/">Prof. Arthur W. Toga</a> at the <a href="https://keck.usc.edu/">Keck School of Medicine, University of Southern California</a>, where my research centered on developing and improving AI/ML models for biomedical data to better understand the mechanisms of Alzheimer's disease.</p>
  <p>I received my Ph.D. at Tsinghua University in 2024, supervised by <a href="https://www.me.tsinghua.edu.cn/en/info/1051/1325.htm">Prof. Linhong Ji</a>. During my Ph.D., I was a Visiting Scholar at Harvard Medical School and the <a href="https://ltnr.mgh.harvard.edu/">Laboratory for Translational Neurorecovery</a> of <a href="https://www.massgeneral.org/doctors/20937/david-lin">David Lin, MD</a>, at Massachusetts General Hospital in 2023. My Ph.D. thesis focused on designing and applying artificial intelligence methods to predict and uncover stroke patients' motor recovery based on neurological changes.</p>
  <p>I earned my master's degree at George Washington University in 2020, supervised by <a href="https://bme.engineering.gwu.edu/murray-loew">Prof. Murray Loew</a> at the <a href="https://loewlab.seas.gwu.edu/">Medical Imaging &amp; Image Analysis Laboratory</a>. My master's work focused on AI-based medical imaging for tumor segmentation.</p>
</section>
